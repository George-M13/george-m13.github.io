6 Shots

Second year university project

Project Overview

6 Shots was a game I developed with my team in my second year of university—a third-person shooter inspired by the Borderlands series, built in Unity.

I took on the role of Lead Programmer, handling multiple key aspects of the game. For AI, I implemented a goal-driven behavior system for enemies, creating dynamic and engaging encounters. On the gameplay side, I developed core mechanics such as gunplay, looting, and movement. I also worked on shader programming, creating the cel-shaded visual effect to match the game’s stylized aesthetic.

Despite being a semester-long project, 6 Shots remains one of my favorite projects due to its strong Borderlands influence, a series I love. Our team followed an Agile methodology, using weekly sprints, backlog management, and regular scrums, which helped us achieve a high-quality result within a short timeframe.

Key Features

  • Goal-Driven AI Behaviour System
  • Third Person Shooter Mechanics
  • Cel-Shaded Aesthetic Shaders
